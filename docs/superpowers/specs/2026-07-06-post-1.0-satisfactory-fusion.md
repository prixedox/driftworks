## 0. Why Satisfactory, and what actually transfers

Factorio's DNA gave 1.0 its systems (logistics, ratios, pollution/defense). Satisfactory's
DNA is different: **charm, discovery, and player-verbs** — the world is a character, the
factory is a place you *inhabit*, and progression includes toys, not just throughput.
Its most-loved features cluster into four transferable groups:

1. **Discovery-driven mastery** — hard drives → **alternate recipes** (community-verified
   design principle: they work because they're *situational alternatives, not strict
   upgrades*), power slugs → **overclocking shards**. Both reward exploration with
   production depth — a perfect match for our biome/POI world.
2. **Overflow → delight** — the AWESOME Sink turns excess into coupons and a shop.
   Solves the late-game "storage full" dead-end AND gives cosmetics a home (our
   monetization stance: cosmetics only, never pay-to-win).
3. **Motion as fun** — hypertubes, jump pads, ziplines. Traversal is a toy. Our
   player-is-a-character pillar is begging for this.
4. **Charm** — ADA's voice, lizard doggos, photo mode. Near-zero mechanical cost,
   outsized personality and marketing value (doggo clips built Satisfactory's social
   presence).

What does NOT transfer: first-person verticality (foundations, multi-floor factories) —
wrong camera, wrong input; we take the *spirit* (elevated belts) and skip the rest.

## 1. Update 1.1 — “Charm & Flow” (small, ~1 phase of work)

**1.1a The Reclaimer (AWESOME Sink analog).** A 1×1 building that consumes ANY solid item
from belts and converts it to **Scrip** by an `ITEM_VALUE` table (raw = 1, plate-tier = 4,
circuit-tier = 12, adv-tier = 40, science = 25 — geometric with tier, tuned so sinking
raw ore is wasteful and sinking finished goods is respectable). Scrip is a sim counter
(deterministic), spent in a **Scrip Shop** (menu tab): machine skins, packet trail
effects, player colorways — pure presentation, applied client-side, persisted in the save
envelope. Sim cost: one machine + counter. UI: shop panel. *Solves overflow, funds charm.*

**1.1b Skyway belts (verticality-lite).** A belt-bridge module: one placed unit spanning
exactly one tile of anything (belt, pipe, wall, lava), rendered raised on pylons with
packets arcing over. Complements undergrounds (which span 4 but cost gears and hide the
packets — Skyways keep the Pulse look visible; *situational alternative, not upgrade*).
Sim: transport module with a fixed 2-slot internal path; render: raised mesh + packet
Y-offset.

**1.1c The Scrapling (lizard-doggo analog).** A skittish critter found near ruins (one
per biome, POI-adjacent). Approach slowly (walk speed < 50% within 4 tiles — client
detects, sim validates a `tame` command cooldown) → it follows you and periodically digs
up a gift (small salvage roll every ~5 min, `loot`-style sim command with a deterministic
tick cooldown; the *timing prompt* is client-side, the *grant* is sim-side). One active
Scrapling; it idles at your base when dismissed; skins in the Scrip Shop. *Marketing
gold; near-zero sim surface.*

**1.1d VECTOR (ADA analog).** The tutorial/objective voice becomes a character: a dry,
politely sinister logistics AI. A `VECTOR_LINES` data table keyed by event (tutorial
steps, first raid, storm, launch, sinking science into the Reclaimer — it *disapproves*),
rendered as a styled toast variant with a typing effect. Text only in 1.1 (TTS never;
recorded VO maybe post-2.0). *Personality for the cost of a string table.*

## 2. Update 1.2 — “Mastery” (the depth update, ~1–2 phases)

**1.2a Data Cores → alternate recipes (hard-drive analog).** New POI kind: **wrecks**
(crashed drift-vessels, 2 per biome, guarded in Drifter by a stalker pack). Looting
yields a `data_core` item. A new **Decoder** building (or a Lab tab — decide at plan
time) consumes one core over 60 s and offers **a choice of 3** alternate recipes drawn
deterministically from `ALT_RECIPES` via `hash2(seed, coreIndex)`; the player picks one
to unlock permanently (choice is a command; offer is reproducible). The table (~12
recipes) follows the *situational-alternative* rule — each trades a scarce input for an
abundant one or shifts WHERE production happens, never strictly better. Seed examples:
`alt_cast_gear` (1 steel → 4 gear — steel-rich bases), `alt_crystal_circuit`
(1 crystal + 1 wire → 1 circuit — Hollows outposts), `alt_coal_plastic` (3 coal →
1 plastic, no oil — pre-canopy plastic at brutal coal cost), `alt_heavy_frame`
(1 steel + 4 gear → 1 frame — gear-spam route), `alt_fuel_synthesis` (2 coal + 1 water →
1 drift_fuel at 3× time — desperation fuel). Recipe-select UI already exists; alternates
appear in machines' recipe pickers + the codex with an "ALT" badge.

**1.2b Overclocking — Drift Shards (power-slug analog).** `drift_shard` item: rare —
crafted (3 refined_crystal + 1 science4 → 1, late-game sink) AND found in salvage/wreck
loot (early taste). Inspector gains an overclock row on production machines: install
1/2 shards → 150%/200% speed at **160%/250% power draw** (integer tables; the
superlinear power cost is the balancing lever, exactly Satisfactory's). Shards are
recoverable on erase. Sim: per-module `shards: 0|1|2`, rate + draw scaling in the
existing upgrade-aware locals. *Gives the optimizer a per-machine dial and makes power
matter again in the endgame.*

## 3. Update 1.3 — “Motion” (~1 phase)

**1.3a Transit tubes (hypertube analog).** `tube` segments (pipe-like auto-connecting
runs) + `tube_station` endpoints. Walk onto a powered station → glide along the tube path
at 4× walk speed with a swoosh (client-side traversal over the sim-owned tube path;
player position stays out of the sim, unchanged). Costs steel + circuits; stations draw
class-1 power. *The factory becomes a transport network you ride — pure Satisfactory joy,
and it makes cross-biome logistics bases feel connected.*

**1.3b Jump pads (lite).** A cheap directional pad that flings the walking player 6 tiles
(client-side arc; sim only validates placement). Drifter synergy: escape routes.

**1.3c Photo mode.** HUD-off + free camera pan/zoom (temporarily decoupled from the
player) + optional day-phase override (render-side only) + PNG export/share. *UGC engine
for the communities that carried both Factorio's and Satisfactory's word-of-mouth.*

## 4. Update 2.0 — unchanged

Co-op lockstep, app stores, blueprint sharing — `docs/ROADMAP.md` Horizon 5 as written.
Everything above is deliberately co-op-safe: every new mechanic is command-driven and
deterministic; cosmetics/photo/traversal are client-side.

## 5. Architecture & determinism notes (binding on all of the above)

- Every grant/consume (scrip, gifts, cores, shard installs, alt-recipe picks) is a
  **command**; every offer/roll uses `hash2` off the seed — replays and future co-op
  stay intact. Client-side: traversal, camera, cosmetics, timing prompts.
- All content lands as data tables (`ITEM_VALUE`, `ALT_RECIPES`, `VECTOR_LINES`,
  `SHARD_TABLES`, shop catalog) + narrow sim seams — the `economy_graph` net extends to
  alternates (an ALT recipe's inputs must be producible; its unlock is a data_core
  choice, modeled like a tech unlock).
- Save: v5 bump at 1.1 (scrip, scrapling, skins), additive fields after (alt unlocks,
  shards, tubes). Migration chain per the established pattern.
- Mobile budget still governs: Skyways/tubes add meshes — instanced from day one
  (Phase 11 infra exists by then).

## 6. Sequencing rationale

1.1 first because it's small, ships personality fast post-launch (retention window), and
the Reclaimer fixes the first complaint every idle-adjacent audience files (overflow).
1.2 is the depth statement for the genre audience — time it with the first content
review wave. 1.3 rides on 1.1's polish and feeds marketing (photo mode + tubes = clips).
Each update = its own spec→plan cycle; this document is the spec seed for all three.
